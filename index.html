Safety Through Technology

Wearable Technology enables empowerment of women where legislation, policy making and the law enforcements have lacked. There are several companies out there making this technology but how can it really help when the costs are too high, or the application of it, for example in a domestic case is virtually nil.

ImSafe community strategy will intervene here. With the money we raise, we will buy a viable rape protection technology and then donate them to local organizations who can effectively utilize the tool, specific to the incidents in their communities.

​ There are places around the world where a woman does not feel comfortable calling the police. ​Perhaps instead of alerting the police, a woman can alert an ally within her family. Or can seek an ally in the local organization itself.

There is also still a huge gap between what is available in the market and the need of the women, these are affordability and versatility. Our ImSafe Product strategy will not only fill the gaps but drive the convergence between the two through technology enabled sensors, digitally contoured optical lenses for image capturing and leveraging bluetooth for connectivity.

We will continuously use the latest technology that will keep the ​vulnerable and the weak safe.

Executing the Vision

Im Safe is determined to executing the vision by taking small yet efficient steps in connecting to one organization at a time.

One of our first goals is raising funds to be able to afford technology and supporting education outreach. We have partnered with a trading app for clothes called MyKlosets where in 100% of the proceeds from customer’s donated clothes will be used for ImSafe funding.

In parallel, we are partnering with organization in India called DURGA. They are based out of Bangalore, India and are making great strides in educating women on how to be in control about their safety.

The goal of DURGA is to empower young women to become champions, mentors and facilitators; so that they can be equipped with practical skills that can help them take on everyday harassment bravely. Through interactive theatre games embedded into a 3-hour module that assesses and helps in understanding sexual harassment, an individual’s social environment and his or her response mechanism, Durga will help in distinguishing and responding to challenging situations in a simple, yet effective way. So far, DURGA has successfully touched the lives of nearly 1,000 women across Bangalore, and in the days to come, this number will certainly grow. For more information: Website: www.durgaindia.org

Our next goal is to partner with an organization that educates both men and women and disrupting the deep seated beliefs of male power dynamics within a community that allow rape to even occur in the first place. Since this approach is a harder road, we want to take the time to partner with a great one. About Us

Rape. It’s no longer just a crisis, but a global emergency. Not only is it growing in numbers by the day, all the emotional underpinnings and the cultures surrounding it are complex. What can we do to stop this? Certainly, this isn’t a new question. However, the ImSafe team is determined to find solutions that eradicate causes of sexual violence and it’s effects.

We are a non-profit organization that wants to connect and work together with various organizations to provide access to ensure safety of women all over the world. This means, working regionally and sub regionally to decide what approaches are best within each community of men and women. We are the knot that ties the anti-rape campaigns and organizations together. We will begin with education and technology at every level and work our way through the cast net.

Lata Sharma

Change happened to me too...when in 1977 I heard, between sobs, from my best friend that her boyfriend had raped her...I did not know how to help and just cried with her ... change happened to me in Dec 16th 2012 when 'Nirbhaya' was raped in Delhi, India. Again...I felt I could only cry with rest of the women in India. Something swept through me as the discussion in the media frustrated me. I was angry. How long will I stand on the sideline and do nothing. I decided it is Now...it is time for CHANGE! It is time for ACTION! I left my corporate career of 13 years to be the change.

I know, we all have stories like this, stories or events that saw a change in us. It is time to drive the change as a collective force, as a community ... as a wave that will give safety to many women. Come join us and lets become a force. I am not even sure where we will go from here. Except that I know I have several dots and with your help I will be able to connect all of them.
